SHADE OF COLORS
A barely imperceptible colored-speck that exists around the host character. The color permeates an aura around the host, which rapidly change color when they use their shade technique. A shade that holds unspeakable power despite its simplicity, Shade of Colors is able to manipulate the properties and states of the matter around it.
PASSIVE

Yellow encapsulates the flow of Spirit energy in the world and it's creatures. Yellow rapidly shifts and manipulates the Spirit of creatures, essentially bending it's will and it's life force. As a passive, you regenerate 1SP at the start of your turn

Golden Yellow has granted you near perfect control over your own spirit output, after a long rest, you can decrease SP costs of any of your abilities by up to 3x your Proficiency Bonus, you can allocate the reductions however you want (decrease purple by 9/ decrease amp blue, red, purple by 3 each). Additionally, your maximum SP cap increases by 4

Your Spirit Defense and Armor Class increases by 1. Additionally, your maximum SP increases by 5. Additionally, you gain access to maximum techniques of your base color abilities. As a free action on your turn or as a reaction, you can change your existing ongoing Maximum Technique.

Finally, your signature is now on a 1d6 turns cooldown rather than once per long rest.

SHADE ABILITY

When touching an object, you can rapidly displace or add energy to it. For example, producing a fireball by holding a lit flame and rapidly adding heat energy to it, or rapidly taking away gravitational potential energy from an object, preventing it from falling momentarily as it gathers it's potential energy again. This technique is heavily SP intensive, costing 6SP per 6 seconds a technique is active. Additionally, as a bonus action, you can add additional magical damage to a spell cast equal to ⅕ of SP used.

As a bonus action, you can add red into ONE INSTANCE of the effect of a spell or a cantrip that you cast in the same turn. (Ala, scorching ray is 3 rays, but only 1 ray has red). When using red, the spell or cantrip will target the enemy using it's Spirit Defense stat rather than its Armor Class if it's an attack roll. Red also allows the spell or cantrip to ignore cover and line of sight, as well as extending the range of the ability by 30ft. By expending another 3SP for a total of 9SP, you can enhance the effect of red on the spell/cantrip and deal an additional d10 damage, and you can change the damage type of the ray to any damage type (except true).

As a main action, you manifest and amplify an expression of red into a blast of pure spirit and energy. Target a creature within 120ft, you throw your Amplified Red towards them, a mass of immense spirit and energy. Make a spell attack roll against the creature, they take 2d10 + (Proficiency Bonus)d10 damage, this damage can be any type you choose

By rapidly shifting energy and SP, the host can transmute simple materials, such as a simple block of wood, carbon etc. Additionally, by consuming an exorbitant amount of SP, the host may attempt to transmute away solid objects, but the rate at which is extremely slow and SP inefficient. The more complex the object, the more SP it takes.

As a bonus action, you can add blue into ONE INSTANCE of the effect of a spell or a cantrip that you cast in the same turn. (Ala, scorching ray is 3 rays, but only 1 ray has red). When using blue, you are able to delay the casting and activation of spells and cantrips, storing the spell and cantrip within an item, surface, object or materialise the spell/cantrip as a barely physical blue orb. The spell will automatically trigger when the object or surface is destroyed, and if not, they are automatically casted at the start of your next turn. By expending another 3SP for a total of 9SP, you can enhance the effect of blue on the spell/cantrip to deal an additional d10 damage and ignores resistances.

As a main action, you manifest and amplify an expression of blue into a blast of pure spirit and molecular matter. Target a range within 120ft, you throw your Amplified Blue, which will galvanize and transmute all matter within the area destructively, releasing a heavy blast of energy. Every creature within a 20ft radius of the centre of Amplified Blue must make a Dexterity save or take 5d6 + (Proficiency Bonus)d6. This damage ignores resistances

SHADE SIGNATURE

As an action, the host character picks a simple object no bigger than the palm of his hand, the object is rapidly compressed and charged with energy by Blue and Red. The object slowly becomes a glowing purple orb which can be launched at any target within 400ft. Every creature the orb passes, make an attack roll against the creature. The orb will punch through any simple physical barriers and deals (2 + Proficiency Bonus)d10 kinetic damage to any creature successfully hit by the orb.

Every creature successfully hit by the attack will be debuffed with one of TWO debuffs:

  • Red: Creature is unable to move until the end of their next turn, as their energy is rapidly sapped away
  • Blue: Creatures physical armor and resistances is rapidly deconstructed. Creature cannot benefit from resistances until the end of their next turn
After using purple, roll a d6, on a 4 or higher, signature usage is not consumed

As an action, the host character picks a simple object no bigger than the palm of his hand, the object is rapidly compressed and charged with energy by Amplified Blue and Amplified Red. As part of the same action, you can freely convert as many spell slots as you can into Spirit, and expend as much SP on this ability as you want, with the minimum of 28. The object slowly becomes a glowing purple orb which can be launched at any target within 400ft, 10ft wide. Every creature the orb passes, make an attack roll against the creature. The orb will punch through any simple physical barriers and deals (3 + 2 * Proficiency Bonus)d12 force damage and deal additional damage equal to SP spent to any creature successfully hit by the orb.

Creatures hit by Perfect Purple incur BOTH the following effects.

  • Red: Creature is unable to move until the end of their next turn, as their energy is rapidly sapped away
  • Blue: Creatures physical armor and resistances is rapidly deconstructed. Creature cannot benefit from resistances until the end of their next turn
After Perfect Purple is finished casting, host is unable to regain SP for two turns, and their shade abilities are disabled for one turn.
